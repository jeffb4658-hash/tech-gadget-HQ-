What to Look for in a Mechanical Keyboard

Before we dive into our top picks, let's discuss what makes a great mechanical keyboard. Here are some key factors to consider:

  • Switch type: Mechanical keyboards use physical switches under each key. Popular switch types include Cherry MX Red, Brown, and Blue.
  • Keycap quality: Look for keyboards with durable, well-balanced keycaps that provide a smooth typing experience.
  • Keyboard layout: Consider a keyboard with a standard QWERTY layout, or explore alternative layouts like tenkeyless or ergonomic designs.
  • Connectivity options: Opt for a keyboard with multiple connectivity options, such as USB, Bluetooth, or wireless.
